ALL DONE WITH 3 EASY STEPS

1 Step Notary Attestation

Your document is first verified and stamped by a registered notary to confirm its authenticity.

2 Step SDM Attestation

Next, the Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) attests the notary-verified document. This is a mandatory step before apostille.

3 Step Apostille from MEA

Finally, the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) issues the Apostille sticker, making your document valid for use in Hague Convention countries.

Frequently Asked Questions

An apostille is an official certificate that verifies the authenticity of a public document for use in foreign countries. Issued under the Hague Convention of 1961, it removes the need for embassy attestation. In India, the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) provides apostille services for documents like birth, marriage, education, or power of attorney. It confirms the seal and signature, making the document valid abroad for immigration, education, work, or legal use.
