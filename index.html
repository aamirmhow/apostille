What is an Apostille?

An apostille is a certificate issued by a designated authority in a country that is a member of the Hague Convention. It authenticates the origin of a public document—such as a birth certificate, marriage certificate, educational degree, or legal affidavit—so it can be recognized internationally. Apostille services are essential for individuals who wish to use their documents abroad for purposes like visa applications, job opportunities, higher education, or immigration. Instead of going through long legalization processes at embassies, an apostille simplifies it by verifying the document for global acceptance. In India, the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) is the primary authority responsible for issuing apostilles. Once your document is apostilled, it is legally valid in all countries that are part of the Hague Apostille Convention, without the need for further verification. This saves time, reduces stress, and ensures your paperwork meets international standards.
