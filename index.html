What is an Apostille?

An apostille is an official certificate that verifies the authenticity of a public document for use in foreign countries. Issued under the Hague Convention of 1961, it removes the need for embassy attestation. In India, the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) provides apostille services for documents like birth, marriage, education, or power of attorney. It confirms the seal and signature, making the document valid abroad for immigration, education, work, or legal use.
